EQ Design Notes

--
I want to design for:
-Bypass Switch for easy comparison/low noise
-Fixed freq HPF/LPF for speaker protection
-Fixed freq LF/HF shelving for broad tone control
-2 Fully parametric middle bands for specific controls
-Sweepable Notch filter for cleaning noise

This exposes me to many different filter designs while being highly functional
---
Power: This should only be used when it's near outlets, so it makes sense to have some sort of wall wart, clean up or modify the signal, and make power rails. 15dB is standard max boost. 15dB = 5.62x, so ±15V rails = ±13V output = 2.3V peak input. Seems reasonable. Batteries seems inconvenient and bulk, USB voltage levels considered, but I'm worried about signal integrity, noise, power drain, etc. Not interested in designing power solution right now. Ne5532 draws 8mA max per channel and the TL072B draws 2.5mA max per channel. Likely need a 1W supply.
--
Goal is low resistance, linear, single gang pots. Avoid rotary switches ($5-$10).
Note that compromises made on freq range selection, as changing C in 1/2piRC only shifts the line up and down, not slope freq vs R loglog graphs.
--
Pre-amp not needed because the speakers will handle volume control. 
--
E24 values preferred for components since easier find and I have E24 metal film resistors. E6 caps though. I want metal film resistors default. X5R/X7R caps for decoupling, C0G(NP0) or Polypropylene (>1nF) caps for filters. 1kohm - 4.7kohm seems to be commonplace in audio design, due to balance noise, current, and parasitics. Try to keep cap values below 1u. Otherwise, consider bipolar electrolytic bypassed with PP film.

resistor noise prop. to  sqrt(R)

On decoupling. 100nF close to package across supply rails for NE5532/TL072, not two going to ground to avoid noise.

NE5532P default, solid option at a cheap price (5534 is x2, lm4562 x12). Input current bias on the NE5532 is 200-1000nA, 65-7000pA on the TL072, based on typical at 25C to full range temp. This becomes a problem with bigger resistors (do voltage/current noise calculations), so switch to the TL072 around 20kohm. TL072H might be best bc EMI filters, slew rate, and low power. TL072A/B have tighter input offset voltage. Right now TL072BCP seems to be best bang for buck.
